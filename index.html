Related Topics & Outlines

💡 Clinical Pearls & Learning Points

  • A detailed ECG must be performed and interpreted within 10 minutes of presentation for suspected ACS.
  • In DKA management, insulin therapy must be held if serum potassium is below 3.3 mEq/L.
  • For renal protection in hypertensive diabetic patients, ACE inhibitors or ARBs are first-line.

⚠️ Clinical Red Flags & Diagnostic Tips

  • Red Flag: Hypotension, confusion, and cold clammy skin in MI indicate cardiogenic shock—avoid venodilators like nitroglycerin.
  • Red Flag: Hypokalemia in DKA—replacing potassium before starting insulin is mandatory to avoid cardiac arrest.
  • Tip: Geriatric and diabetic patients frequently present with silent MI (unexplained dyspnea, fatigue, epigastric discomfort).

📚 Related Cases & Quick Revision Notes

Atypical Acute Coronary Syndromes Understand the pathophysiology of autonomic neuropathy causing silent ischemia in long-standing diabetes.
Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (MUDPILES) DKA is a hallmark cause of high anion gap acidosis due to beta-hydroxybutyrate accumulation.
