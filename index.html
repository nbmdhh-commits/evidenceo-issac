How do I distinguish between respiratory acidosis and metabolic compensation?
Look at the direction of pH change, then check if pCO₂ or HCO₃⁻ explains it. If pH is low (acidemia) and pCO₂ is high, it's primary respiratory acidosis. Metabolic compensation is active if HCO₃⁻ has risen above normal limits to buffer the pH.
What are the compensation formulas for acute vs chronic respiratory acidosis?
• Acute: HCO₃⁻ rises by 1 mEq/L for every 10 mmHg increase in pCO₂.
• Chronic: HCO₃⁻ rises by 3.5 mEq/L for every 10 mmHg increase in pCO₂.
Let's run a clinical simulation: A patient presents with pH 7.25, pCO₂ 60 mmHg, and HCO₃⁻ 26 mEq/L. Is this acute or chronic respiratory acidosis?
