Cardiology Core

Overall Mastery 74%

Definition

Integrative study of the heart and circulatory system, combining cardiac anatomy, electrical signaling, coronary diseases, and hemodynamics.

Pathophysiology

Cardiovascular dysfunction arises from perfusion deficits, electrical conduction defects, or mechanical pump failure.

Clinical Features

Chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations, syncope, lightheadedness, and signs of poor peripheral perfusion.

Recognition Rules

Evaluate hemodynamic status (BP, consciousness). Obtain immediate 12-lead ECG and assess QRS width, rate, and rhythm regularity.

Clinical Pearl: Always assess hemodynamics first. Any unstable rhythm requires immediate electricity, not drugs.
Exam Trap: Never administer calcium channel blockers (Verapamil) to wide-complex tachycardias.
